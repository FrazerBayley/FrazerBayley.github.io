Frazer Bayley

I fell in love with programming in my very first term of college. I took an introductory HTML and CSS class where I discovered the power a few lines of code could possess. I declared my major as Computer and Information Science as soon as I could do so and haven’t looked back since.

Ever since then I have been hungry to learn more about programming and Computer Science. I first learned Python, then I was introduced to Java and thrown into the land of object oriented languages. Quickly after I was tasked with learning C, after a few weeks and months of banging my head against the keyboard I grew more comfortable with its unforgiving nature. As of today I would say C is my favorite programming language, it is at times a love/hate relationship, but the feeling that one gets after finding and fixing a memory leak after hours of searching is hard to match.

I’ve had some great teachers at the University of Oregon. Teachers that have shown me how to create precise, fast and most important of all – readable code. People who helped me to understand things like Dijkstra’s algorithm and Red-Black trees.

I have experience working in multiple Software Companies as an intern. In the summer of 2016 I worked alongside the BI Development team at FusionOps Inc. A company based in Mountain View, CA whose product supplied companies a view into their SAP supply chain data. My tasks included validating data between development cycles by writing and running SQL query’s. I also set up infrastructure to record the usage of the FusionOps product, which the upper level executives at FusionOps look at to see how the customer uses their product.

I am a quick learner and possess the ability to teach myself new technologies. I tackle hard programming problems the way I tackle large mountains when I backpack; I never stop trying, I never stop moving forward. Eventually you will make it to the top, and the rewards will be endless.
